About

I am a veteran member of Scouting Ireland and Mountaineering Ireland for over 22 years. I have travelled around the world, exploring the landscapes of europe. I grew up a stone's throw away from Larch Hill, a national scouting centre of 35 hectares and many facilities for the young and the old in scouting. They also help schools to organise their Gaisce hikes during the weeks.


I wanted to start a walking club because I realised the number of people getting involved with the outdoor was decreasing at an extreme rate. From when I was young, I learnt the simple pleasure in walking for a few hours to separate myself from daily life as well as an appreciation for the views that just a little bit of effort can bring.
My aim with this walking club is to encourage old and young people to engage with their surroundings while also promoting healthy living.
